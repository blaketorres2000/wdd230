Alignment

Wedevs

wedevs.com

There are many factors when considering the alignment of items on a webpage. Wedevs' website uses a vast combination of these elements. The homepage has several examples of horizontal and vertical alignment. The page is well-centered, and pictures do not distract you too much from the text. Even as you scroll down the page, your eyes stay centered while looking at each item. This keeps the user from getting too distracted and keeps the user engaged and interested.

Contrast

Panic

panic.com

From the text to the colors to shapes, Panic's website is a perfect example of contrast. Everything on their page stands out on its own. There is no doubt that the contrast theme was intentional. The padding around each item allows for that item to contrast against the background. The sizes of the text and pictures give plenty of contrast to each individual element. The colors are bold, yet it is not overwhelming, thus allowing you to see each item clearly.

White Space and Clean Design

Apple

apple.com

Apple's website is a prime example of white space because of the simplicity of its website. There are not many things on the page, but how the white space draws your attention to the items they are trying to highlight is enticing. It allows for the items on the page to almost jump off of the page. This example of white space helps me to realize that starting with too much white space and slowly narrowing it down to what the page needs are the best approaches to building a website with white space in mind.
