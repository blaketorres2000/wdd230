Discover Fred, Texas

Alabama Coushatta Tribe of Texas

The Alabama-Coushatta Tribe of Texas is a Federally recognized tribe of Alabama and Koasati in Polk County, Texas. Descendants of the historic Alabama and Coushatta tribes of Indians, who merged in the 18th century, they are members of the federally recognized Coushatta Tribe of Louisiana and the Alabama-Quassarte Tribal Town of Oklahoma. The tribe hosts an annual powwow in June.

Big Thicket National Preserve

Big Thicket National Preserve is a large nature preserve encompassing nine different ecosystems, from longleaf pine forests to cypress-lined bayous. It's home to a diverse range of animal and plant life, including carnivorous pitcher plants. The preserve's 100+ miles of hiking trails include the 20-mile-long Big Thicket Trail. The preserve is also popular for canoeing and kayaking on the Neches River.

The Heritage Museum

The Heritage Village Museum is a collection of historic buildings from the late 1800s and early 1900s. The buildings were moved to the museum site from various locations in Polk County. The museum is located on the grounds of the Polk County Commerce Center, 101-C West Church Street, Livingston, Texas.

Village Creek State Park

Village Creek State Park is a 1,090-acre park located in Hardin County, Texas, near Lumberton and approximately 10 miles north of Beaumont. The park is located along Village Creek, which flows into the Neches River. The park was opened in 1994.

Martin Dies Jr. State Park

Martin Dies Jr. State Park is a 705.9-acre state park along the banks of the Steinhagen Reservoir on U.S. Highway 190, approximately 10 miles west of Jasper, Texas. The park is maintained by the Texas Parks and Wildlife Department.
